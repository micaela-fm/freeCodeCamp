Metal subgenres

Metal music spans a vast range of styles, each offering its own mood, themes, and sound. This page explores three distinct subgenres: the crushing weight of doom metal, the raw intensity of black metal, and the atmospheric depth of folk metal. Dive in and discover what makes each of these styles unique.

Doom Metal

Doom metal is characterized by slow tempos, heavy riffs, and a dark, melancholic atmosphere. It often explores themes of despair, loss, and existential dread, creating a powerful and immersive listening experience.

Black Metal

Black metal is known for its fast pace, shrieking vocals, and raw production. It often delves into themes of nature, mythology, and rebellion, delivering an intense and atmospheric sound that is both aggressive and haunting.

Folk Metal

Folk metal blends traditional folk music with heavy metal, incorporating instruments like flutes and violins alongside guitars and drums. It celebrates cultural heritage and mythology, creating epic and melodic soundscapes.
